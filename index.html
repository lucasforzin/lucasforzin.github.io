05 — Projects

Network Analysis for Mobility and Transportation

Strategic Resource Allocation During Strikes: the Venice Navigation System Case

Politecnico di Milano — with ACTV (Azienda del Consorzio Trasporti Veneziano)

A network analysis of the ACTV water transport system in Venice — 16 lines and 65 aggregated piers serving around 95 million passengers a year. The project reconstructs an origin-destination matrix from partial boarding/alighting data through a quadratic optimization model, then analyzes the network at macro, micro and meso scale (degree, strength, betweenness, closeness, k-coreness and s-core centrality) to identify the most critical nodes — Ferrovia, Piazzale Roma and San Zaccaria.

The second part of the study addresses how to reallocate limited resources during strikes, comparing four strategies — a Combined Centrality Score, a Criticality Index, Commuter Flow Centrality, and a uniform reduction — for both partial frequency cuts and full line closures, while preserving lagoon-wide connectivity.

Python — scipy.optimize (L-BFGS-B) PTV Visum GTFS data Network centrality

Mobility: Infrastructures and Services

Urban Public Transport and Construction Sites — Redevelopment of Piazza dei Cinquecento, Rome

Politecnico di Milano — with NET Engineering International

A study on pedestrian accessibility during the redevelopment of Piazza dei Cinquecento, Rome's largest intermodal hub in front of Termini station — an intervention tied to the Jubilee 2025 timeline. The project segments demand into tourists (65%) and commuters (35%), maps points of interest within a 1,200 m walking radius, and uses QGIS and OpenStreetMap to build the pedestrian network.

The four real construction phases were grouped into two analytical blocks to simulate, in PTV Visum, which pedestrian paths are disrupted at each stage. The results show that while Phase 1 has only a minor impact, Phase 2 blocks 8.3% of paths and increases average walking time from 5.7 to 10.6 minutes — with the largest delays for universities, schools, offices and museums.

PTV Visum QGIS OpenStreetMap Demand segmentation Pedestrian accessibility

Olympic Games 2026: New Integrated and Sustainable Connections from Milan to Livigno

Politecnico di Milano

Public Transport Management · 2025 · Project Work with Dolomitibus

Design of Urban Bus Network in Belluno Municipality

Politecnico di Milano — with Dolomitibus

A complete design of a new urban public transport network for the Municipality of Belluno, starting from the analysis of the main demand corridors and trip attractors in the city — such as the hospital, the railway station and the main residential areas. The project defined eight bus lines, simulating different operational models based on headway and on timetable design, in addition to evaluating an innovative scenario with Demand Responsive Transport (DRT) services. The analysis was completed with an economic study to verify the operational sustainability of the system and identify the most efficient solution in terms of service level, fleet utilization and operating costs.

06 — Bachelor's Thesis

Electric Mobility and New Models of Cities

UNIVERSITY OF TRIESTE — 2022/2023

Mobilità elettrica e i nuovi modelli di città

My bachelor's thesis examines the shift from internal combustion to electric vehicles — their environmental, economic and technical advantages and limitations — and places it within a broader rethinking of urban mobility models.

It covers smart cities, Barcelona's Superblock model, the "Città 30" (30 km/h city) approach to street design and road safety, and the rise of shared mobility and Mobility as a Service (MaaS) as drivers of more sustainable, people-centered cities.
